This guide will help you start using Chat, Prisme.ai's secure conversational interface for enterprise AI. Whether you're new to AI or experienced with similar tools, this walkthrough will get you up and running quickly.

## Accessing Chat

<Steps>
  <Step title="Log in to Prisme.ai">
    Access your organization's Prisme.ai instance using your credentials.

    <Frame>
      ![Prisme.ai Login Screen](/images/login-screen.png)
    </Frame>
    If you don't have access credentials, contact your organization's Prisme.ai administrator.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Chat">
    In the sidebar, expand the **Use** menu. You'll see two options:
    
    - **Chat** - Start conversations with AI agents
    - **Store** - Discover and browse available agents

    Click **Chat** to open the main conversational interface.

    <Frame>
      ![Navigating to Chat](/images/securechat-navigation.png)
    </Frame>
    Chat is often the default landing page after login.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore the home screen">
    The Chat home screen shows:

    - A welcome message and input box
    - **Prompt categories** - Quick-access pills to browse [ready-made prompts](./prompt-management)
    - **Available agents** - Agents shared with you
    - **Recent canvas** - Your recent content creation work
    - **News** - Updates from your organization
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the Interface

Let's explore the key elements of the Chat interface:

<Frame>
  ![Chat Interface](/images/securechat-home.png)
</Frame>

### Chat Home Screen

When you open Chat, you'll see:

1. **Welcome message** - Personalized greeting with input box
2. **Available agents** - Agent cards you can click to start chatting
3. **Recent canvas** - Quick access to your recent content work
4. **News section** - Updates from your organization

### Conversation View

When chatting with an agent:

1. **Agent sidebar** - List of agents and your conversation history
2. **Model selector** - Choose which AI model to use (top of chat area)
3. **Message area** - Your conversation with the agent
4. **Input box** - Type your messages
5. **Attach file** - Upload documents to analyze
6. **Tools** - Access agent capabilities
7. **Dictate** - Use voice input
8. **Send** - Send your message

## Your First Conversation

Let's start a basic conversation with Chat:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ask a question">
    Type a question or request in the input box and press Enter or click the send button.

    Example questions to get started:

    - "Can you help me draft an email to my team about our upcoming project deadline?"
    - "Summarize the key points about Retrieval Augmented Generation."
    - "What are some best practices for effective virtual meetings?"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the response">
    The AI will generate a response based on your question. The response will appear in the message area.

    You'll see the response appear in real-time as it's being generated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Continue the conversation">
    Follow up with additional questions or requests. Chat maintains context throughout your conversation.

    Example follow-ups:

    - "Can you make that more concise?"
    - "Add a section about [specific topic]."
    - "Rewrite this in a more formal tone."
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Working with Documents

One of Chat's powerful features is the ability to analyze documents:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload a document">
    Click the paperclip icon at the bottom-left of the message composer and pick a document from the file browser.

    <Frame>
      ![Chat file picker open over the conversation view](/images/chat-upload-file-picker.png)
    </Frame>

    {/* TODO screenshot (optional): tight crop of the composer with the paperclip attach icon highlighted. Save as /images/chat-upload-button.png. */}

    Supported formats include PDF, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, text files, and images with text.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for processing">
    The document will be uploaded and processed. This may take a few moments depending on the document size.

    You'll see a progress indicator while the document is being processed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask questions about the document">
    Once processing is complete, you can ask questions specifically about the document content.

    Example document questions:

    - "Summarize the main points of this document."
    - "What are the key findings in the results section?"
    - "Extract the action items from this meeting note."
    - "What data is shown in the table on page 3?"
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Conversations

Keep your Chat experience organized:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a new conversation">
    Click **\+ New chat** in the top-right of the conversation header to start a fresh conversation.

    Starting a new conversation clears the context from previous interactions.

    {/* TODO screenshot: zoom on the top-right "+ New chat" button inside an open conversation. Save as /images/chat-new-chat-button.png. */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rename a conversation">
    Hover any conversation in the sidebar, click the more-options icon that appears on the row, and choose **Rename**. You can also click the conversation title at the top of the message area to rename it inline.

    Use descriptive names that help you identify the conversation topic.

    {/* TODO screenshot: conversation row in the sidebar with its more-options menu open, "Rename" highlighted. Save as /images/chat-rename-conversation.png. */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access conversation history">
    Your past conversations live in the left sidebar, grouped by **Today**, **Yesterday**, **Last 7 days**, **Last 30 days**, and **Older**. Use the search box at the top of the list to find a specific conversation. Collapse or expand the sidebar with the toggle in its bottom-left corner.

    <Frame>
      ![Chat conversation sidebar with grouped history](/images/securechat-conversation-history.png)
    </Frame>

    {/* TODO screenshot: conversation sidebar with multiple groups visible (Today / Yesterday / Last 7 days). Save as /images/chat-conversation-history-list.png. Optional second shot: collapsed sidebar showing the Expand toggle, save as /images/chat-expand-history.png. */}

    Conversations are automatically saved and can be accessed according to your organization's retention policies.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete a conversation">
    Hover a conversation in the sidebar, open its more-options menu, and choose **Delete**. Use **Delete all conversations** at the bottom of the sidebar to clear the full list at once.

    Depending on your organization's settings, deleted conversations may be recoverable for a certain period.

    {/* TODO screenshot: conversation row's more-options menu open with "Delete" highlighted. Save as /images/chat-delete-conversation.png. */}
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Customizing Your Experience

Personalize Chat to suit your needs:

<Accordion title="Model Selection">
  If your organization has multiple AI models available, you can select which one to use:

  1. Click the **Select model** dropdown at the top of the conversation header
  2. Choose from the available models

  {/* TODO screenshot: Select model dropdown opened from the conversation header. Save as /images/chat-model-selection.png. */}

  Different models may have different capabilities, strengths, and usage quotas.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Share conversation">
  Control how and when you share a conversation:

  1. Click the share icon at the top of the conversation header
  2. Generate a read-only link to your conversation
  3. Click the copy icon to copy the link
  4. Share the link with your team

  <Frame>
    ![Share conversation modal with copyable read-only link](/images/securechat-share-dialog.png)
  </Frame>
  Anyone with the link can view the shared conversation; messages you add after sharing remain private to you.
</Accordion>

## Best Practices for Effective Use

  <Card title="Be Specific" icon="bullseye-arrow">

  </Card>
  <Card title="Iterate" icon="arrows-rotate">

  <Card title="Provide Context" icon="circle-info">

</CardGroup>

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="AI response is too long or detailed">
    - Ask for a more concise response
    - Specify the desired length or format
    - Use phrases like "Summarize in 3 bullet points" or "Keep it under 100 words"
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI response is not relevant to my question">
    - Rephrase your question with more specific details
    - Break complex questions into smaller, clearer parts
    - Provide more context about what you're trying to accomplish
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Document processing is taking too long">
    - Check that your document is not excessively large (\>100 pages)
    - Ensure the document format is supported
    - Try splitting very large documents into smaller chunks
    - Check your network connection
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI keeps referencing outdated information">
    - Remember that the AI's knowledge has a cutoff date
    - Provide current information as context in your question
    - Upload recent documents if you need analysis of current data
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
