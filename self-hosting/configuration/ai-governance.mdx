Governe is the control tower of your Prisme.ai environment. It allows **administrators and IT leaders** to monitor, secure, and govern the use of AI across all products and teams.

You can manage:
- Authentication and SSO providers
- RBAC (role-based access control)
- Workspace provisioning and quotas
- Interface customization
- User feedback and platform policies

---

## Initialization

To enable Governe, you must generate a **super admin token**:

<Steps>

<Step title="Step 1: Generate the long-term token">
1. Log in to Prisme.ai with a **super admin** account and open your user profile (select your firstname at the bottom-left corner).  
2. Open "Tokens" page and create a new access token named `ai-governance`.   

**Copy the created token (including the `at:...` prefix), you cannot retrieve it afterwards.**  
</Step>

<Step title="Step 2: Inject the access token">
1. Open **Create > Builder** module and **AI Governance** module.  
2. Open **Settings > Secrets** and fill the created token under the `adminAccessToken` field.  
</Step>
</Steps>

## Interface Personalization

<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Customize Branding & Texts">
- Go to **Interface Customization**
- Update the **logo**, **colors**, and **translations** for all pages
- Personalize sign-in / sign-up / forgot password texts
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Configure SSO Providers">
SSO providers configured at the infrastructure level and in AI Governance are automatically displayed on the sign-in page — nothing to configure on the UI side. Each provider's `label` and `icon` from your config file are used to render the corresponding sign-in button.

You can restrict local authentication with the following environment variables on prismeai-api-gateway :

```json
DISABLE_LOCAL_SIGNIN='true'
DISABLE_LOCAL_SIGNUP='true'
```

- `DISABLE_LOCAL_SIGNIN` disables the local username/password sign-in API.
- `DISABLE_LOCAL_SIGNUP` disables the local sign-up API.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Add Legal & Help Pages">
Under `Interface Customization > Links`, configure:
- Privacy Policy
- Help
- Feedback
- Change Log

You can use custom pages built in Builder with **RichText** or **Form** blocks.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## SSO Access
If you have your own SSO configured, you need to explicitly allow SSO authenticated users to access the **Teams** administration pages :

1. Open **Teams Service** workspace
2. Open **Settings** \> **Advanced**
3. **Manage roles**
4. Add your SSO provider technical name after `prismeai: {}` at the very beginning :

```yaml
authorizations:
  roles:
    editor: {}
    user:
      auth:
        prismeai: {}
        yourOwnSso: {}
```

Access to other pages from Governe itself will automatically be authorized based on users custom role in Governe workspace.

## Roles and Permissions

Governe uses **RBAC** to manage platform access:

- Assign the `PlatformAdmin` role to users for full control
- Use `PlatformManager` to delegate governance tasks
- Refine access to each product under the **Roles** tab

Default roles include `Admin`, `Builder`, `KnowledgeAdmin`, `User`, etc.

To give a user access to Governe:
1. Go to **Users & Permissions**
2. Assign the **PlatformManager** role in the *Manager* column
